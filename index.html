MRI2Speech: Silent Speech Synthesis from Articulatory Movements Recorded by Real-time MRI

Abstract:

Previous speech synthesis models from articulatory movements recorded using real-time MRI (rtMRI) heavily rely on ground-truth audio. Moreover, applying a loss over audio representations like Mel-spectrograms fails to disentangle the content information from the ambient noise and speaker characteristics, leading to poor intelligibility. For the first time, we employ a self-supervised AV-HuBERT network trained using only (video and text) to understand the significance of vocal cord articulators in speech production. With no reliance on ground-truth speech, we perform thorough experiments on two database and demonstrate our methodâ€™s ability to generalize to unseen speakers during training and its ability to synthesize speech in any novel voice of interest. Our method achieved a 15.18% Word Error Rate (WER) on the USC-TIMIT MRI corpus, marking a significant 575.89% improvement over the current state-of-the-art (SOTA).
