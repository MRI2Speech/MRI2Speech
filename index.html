MRI2Speech: Silent Speech Synthesis from Articulatory Movements Recorded by Real-time MRI

Abstract:

Articulators across the vocal tract contain abundant information regarding the human speech production mechanism. Devising a mechanism to decode these articulatory movements to synthesize speech can significantly improve silent speech interactions in numerous applications. We propose fine-tuning a multi-modal self-supervised AV-HuBERT network using (video, text) pairs, leveraging its pre-training on content-rich audio-visual representations. Our approach, which does not rely on a ground-truth audio stream, demonstrates its generalizability to unseen speakers and its capacity to synthesize voice for any target speaker of choice with improved intelligibility. On the USC-TIMIT corpus, the model achieved a 65.65% and 15.18% Word Error Rate (WER) when synthesized in the original speaker's and any target speaker's voice, respectively, compared to the reported 102.6% WER using the existing method. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first study to demonstrate that the rtMRI-based video-to-speech synthesis model generalizes to speakers across two evaluated databases despite diverse conditions in hardware, recording instruments, and illumination.
